Video Quality (VQ) Monitoring Tools and Measurements

In the VQ Lab, our team uses the following tools and measurement techniques to evaluate the video quality of video streams end to end. Some of tools used are:

  • Cheetah V-Factor
  • Tektronix Sentry
  • Tektronix MTS
  • IneoQuest Cricket

The tools analyze the video streams measuring the artifacts found. Measurements are:

  • CONTOURING: Contouring is a defect where abrupt changes between shades of the same color create color bands instead of a gradual change.
  • HALOING: Haloing is typically seen around areas of high contrast, such as sharp lines, text edges, and graphics. On close inspection, part of the graphic appears to extend into the background.
  • MACROBLOCKING: Macroblocking is a defect where the edges of blocks or rows of blocks, are typically seen as a grid-like-pattern. This defect often occurs during dissolves from one scene to another or during action scenes involving a great deal of complex movement.
  • NOISE: Noise appears as random speckles on an otherwise smooth surface and can significantly degrade video quality.
  • SMEARING: Smearing is a defect where part of the image remains fixed in space while the adjacent parts of the image moves leaving a trail.
  • PUMPING: Pumping is a defect where the video or parts of the video appear to pulse at a regular interval.

Mobile Health Application Documentation

Displays current system status to user. The background color of hosts or services will reflect the most severe alarm. In this case, both are red since there is at least one critical/down alarm.

The host and services lists contain all of the current alarms. These can be filtered based on alarm level, host or service name, and date/time range. Basic information is displayed in each cell. This information includes:
  • Current Status
  • Acknowledgement
  • Comments
  • Time of last check
  • Host/Service names
  • Brief description

The details views contain all of the data from the cell as well as comments and a more detailed status message. From here, the details can be shared, acknowledged or commented.

On the subscription views, alarms and hosts can be subscribed to. Based on this subscription, the user will receive the subscribed alarms when they are sent.
